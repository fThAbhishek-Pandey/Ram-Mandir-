Ram Mandir

The Ram Mandir symbolizes spiritual unity, cultural heritage, and the profound devotion of millions of Hindus.

History

The Babri Mosque, built in 1529 in Ayodhya, is believed by many Hindus to be the birthplace of Lord Ram, leading to prolonged disputes. In 2019, the Supreme Court ruled in favor of constructing a Ram temple on the disputed 2.77-acre land and allocated five acres for a mosque elsewhere. The decision was based on evidence of continuous Hindu worship and lack of exclusive Muslim possession. The temple’s construction is seen as a significant resolution, enhancing Ayodhya’s spiritual prominence, boosting the local economy, and fostering social cohesion, while also strengthening India’s cultural diplomacy with countries where Ram is revered.

vision

The vision of the Ram Mandir Trust is to establish the Ram temple in Ayodhya as a global symbol of spiritual unity, cultural heritage, and religious reverence. It aims to create a magnificent temple that honors Lord Ram's legacy, fostering an inclusive environment that promotes social cohesion and national pride. The trust envisions Ayodhya as a vibrant spiritual and cultural hub, attracting pilgrims and tourists worldwide, boosting local development, and enhancing infrastructure. Through educational and charitable initiatives, the trust seeks to contribute to the community's overall growth, embodying the values of peace, prosperity, and cultural diplomacy.

goals

The goal of the Ram Mandir Trust is to oversee the construction and management of the Ram temple in Ayodhya, ensuring it adheres to traditional Hindu architectural and cultural standards. The trust aims to create a spiritual and cultural center that honors the religious significance of Lord Ram's birthplace, promotes social harmony, and fosters economic growth in the region. Additionally, the trust seeks to develop surrounding infrastructure, including charitable institutions like schools and hospitals, enhancing the overall well-being of the community and reinforcing Ayodhya's status as a major pilgrimage and tourist destination.
